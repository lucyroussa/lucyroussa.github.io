Lucy Roussa

Hello! I am a PhD candidate in marine science at North Carolina State University, where I study microplankton community structure in the Gulf of Mexico under the mentorship of Dr. Astrid Schnetzer in the Plankton Ecology Lab. My research is funded by the NOAA Ocean Acidification Program (OAP) with a focus on how environmental conditions influence the carbon biomass and functional diversity of marine plankton. I am passionate about integrating scientific research with education and outreach, inspiring collaboration with local communities to address climate change impacts. By sharing my expertise in algal toxins, marine food webs, and climate change's effects on marine habitats, I aim to empower communities to make informed decisions and advocate for policies that protect marine biodiversity and support resilient coastal economies. I am committed to advancing both scientific knowledge and public engagement, fostering a sense of responsibility for the future of our oceans.
